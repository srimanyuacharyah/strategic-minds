Here's a quick update on what is happening with CivicAI today. We have made some big leaps, and the app is now a living project running right on your machine.

We have finished a lot of things. The app now speaks your language with full support for English, Hindi, and Kannada. Reporting an issue is smarter now since we added AI classification and voice input to make it fast and seamless. We also built a brand new Service Navigator to help you find the right government schemes and bodies. 

We hooked up EmailJS so the app can send actual email updates to users instead of just fake test alerts. We added handy directional buttons like up, down, left, and right to the Admin Dashboard's issue heatmap so you don't have to rely entirely on dragging. We also fixed a glitch that stopped people from signing up if the database keys were invalid, so now it gracefully falls back to local storage and nobody gets blocked.

The landing page and dashboard now show live performance metrics, tracking resolved complaints in real-time. The Admin Console is much more powerful for administrators to manage and track issues. Under the hood, we have fully transitioned to Supabase for our database and Gemini AI for intelligent issue analysis. We also cleaned up the UI by removing duplicate chat widgets and fixing some messy build errors.

Right now, we are working on ensuring that all the new multilingual data and AI reports flow perfectly between the app and Supabase. We are also fine-tuning the layout to ensure the new features look stunning on all devices, and refining the design of the Admin Console and Service Navigator.

Coming up next, we plan to develop a Neighborhood Health Score to track the overall safety of different areas. We are also building a witness system to allow multiple citizens to vouch for an issue to increase its priority, and taking our email alerts to the next level with native mobile or browser push notifications.
